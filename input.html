Input equals output

The beauty of an user story is that it describes a lot in little. An user story is an effective way of notating requirements. It describes the persona for which it will be made, the desired outcome and most importantly the problem that needs to be solved.

But what if the stories on a backlog do not have this proper description? How can we expect the end result, the working software, to do what we expected? It is difficult to have an overview of the quality of a backlog and the statuses of multiple backlogs: the portfolio. This really inflicts with the task of building the best products.

Continuous improvement

By measuring and reporting over the quality of both the individual backlogs as the overall portfolio companies can control their requirement processes much better. Gareth backlog validation provides an overview of the current status as well the improvement over time. This applies for both the company portfolio level as for individual backlogs.

Development teams

Teams can now be sure that the quality of user stories to refine and build are according to their definition of ready by forehand. No more long demotivating meetings where the input is not well prepared but short effective and energizing sprint planning meetings.

Product owners

Product owners must be sure that the highest priority stories are according to the company conventions and there will be no more discrepancies between what is expected and what is delivered.

Product/development management

Gareth gives a clear and concise overview of the current status as well the improvement over time and reporting over the quality of both the individual backlogs as the overall product portfolio.

The benefits

  • No more long demotivating meetings where the input is not prepared well enough but short effective and energizing sprint planning meetings. 
  • No more discrepancies between what is expected and what is delivered.

Configurable

Gareth backlog validation is highly configurable. Multiple issue and project tracking systems like Jira, Trello, Github, Pivotal Tracker are supported with more coming. Gareth distincts the following issue types with their own configurable fill rates:

- user stories (as a, i want, so that | estimations)
- acceptance criteria (given, when, then)
- bugs (reproduction path, expected behaviour, acceptance criteria)
- epics (business goals)
- team tasks

For each of these issue types the threshold and importance can be configured so the results reflects your desired way of working. This way you can be free to set up and enforce your own company conventions regarding to requirements the way you want to.

About backlog validation

Gareths backlog validation creates a concise ranking for your backlog based on the relative importance of your issues. This means that issues in the top of the backlog are weighted more heavily compared to the ones on the bottom. The actual setting is configurable. There are alerts whenever a backlog doesn't meet the quality gate.

Gareth's smart text parsing even enable you to extract issue and criteria content from large free-text fields. This makes it easy to implement in established backlogs or tools with limited custom field support. Gareth can be used as part of your CI environment and can even be integrated into your CI pipeline or on your Jenkins Dashboard.
