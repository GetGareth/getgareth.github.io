  • No more long demotivating meetings but short, effective and energizing sprint planning meetings. 
  • No more discrepancies between what is expected and what is delivered. 
  • No more untracked degradation of the backlog quality.
  • Reporting over the quality of both the individual backlogs as the overall product portfolio. 
  • Enable continuous validation on product and requirement management. 
  • A clear and concise overview of the current status and the improvement over time.
  • Adaptive ranking capabilities to create a simple score. 

Input equals output

The beauty of an user story is that it describes a lot in little. An effective way of notating requirements. It describes the persona for which it will be made, the desired outcome and most importantly the problem that needs to be solved.

But what if the stories on a backlog do not have this proper description? How can we expect the end result, the working software, to do what we expected? It is difficult to have an overview of the quality of a backlog and the statuses of multiple backlogs: the portfolio. This really inflicts with the task of building the best products.

Continuous improvement

By measuring and reporting over the quality of both the individual backlogs as the overall portfolio, companies can control their requirement processes. Gareth backlog validation provides an overview of the current status as well the improvement over time.

Development teams


Teams can now be sure that the quality of user stories to refine and build are according to their definition of ready. No more long demotivating meetings where the input is not well prepared but short effective and energizing sprint planning meetings.

Product owners


Product owners need to make sure that the highest priority stories are according to the company conventions. That way these stories can be estimated and build by the development teams. Gareth gives precise insights in the current status of the backlog and the amount of well defined items for following sprints.

Product/development management

Gareth gives a clear and concise overview of the current status and the improvement over time. Reporting is done over the quality of both the individual backlogs as the overall product portfolio.


Gareth integrates perfectly with:

Highly configurable

Gareth backlog validation is highly configurable. Multiple issue and project tracking systems like Jira, Trello, Github and Pivotal Tracker are supported with more coming. Gareth distinguishes the following issue types with their own configurable fill rates: epics, user stories, bugs, team tasks, improvements.

For each of these issue types the threshold, the criteria and importance can be configured so the results reflect the desired way of working. This way teams have the capability to set up and enforce their companies conventions regarding requirements.

How backlog validation works

Gareths backlog validation creates a concise ranking for your backlog based on the relative importance of your issues. This means that issues in the top of the backlog are weighted more heavily compared to the ones on the bottom. The actual setting is configurable. There are alerts whenever a backlog doesn't meet the quality gate.

Gareth's smart text parsing even enable you to extract issue and criteria content from large free-text fields. This makes it easy to implement in established backlogs or tools with limited custom field support.

Gareth can be used as part of your CI environment and can even be integrated into your CI pipeline and Jenkins dashboard.
